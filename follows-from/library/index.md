# The Library

The library is the side of questions.

It stands for the inward place from which the world is measured, remembered, compared, and read. It does not stand outside the world. It works from inside, with partial traces, incomplete records, inherited signs, measurements, and remembered structures.

The library never possesses the whole. It gathers what reaches it and asks what might make the given understandable.

## What belongs here

- observership
- traces
- memory
- comparison
- interpretation
- legibility
- partial knowledge
- backward reasoning

## Relation to Follows From

The library gives Follows From its philosophical starting point: understanding begins not with possession of the whole, but with the disciplined reading of traces and the questions they force.
