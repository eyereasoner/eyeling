Isolation-breach token — broad constructor-theory coverage case

Answer
YES for the classical isolation-breach token, NO for universal cloning of the quantum provenance seal.

Reason Why
The isolation-breach token is treated as an abstract information variable carried by several unlike classical media: a door beacon, a containment PLC, a nurse pager, and an incident board. Because those substrates are information media for the same variable, the token can be prepared, permuted, reversed, cloned locally, copied across media, measured into an output record, and composed into both serial and parallel task networks. By contrast, the specimen seal is treated as a superinformation medium, so cloning all of its states is impossible.
